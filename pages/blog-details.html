How to Revive a Patchy, Worn-Out Lawn in 5 Steps

James Carter
Senior Horticulturist
March 8, 2026 6 min read

After a harsh winter or period of drought, even the best-kept lawn can show signs of stress. Bare patches, yellowing borders and thinning turf are all common complaints we hear from homeowners in early spring. The good news? Recovery is entirely possible with the right sequence of treatments.

"A lawn isn't just grass — it's a living system. Understand what it needs, and it will respond."

Step 1: Diagnose the Problem

Before you apply anything, identify why the patches exist. Common causes include:

  • Heavy foot traffic compacting the soil
  • Fungal disease (look for circular patterns or grey residue)
  • Pet urine burns (usually circular, yellow-edged patches)
  • Thatch buildup blocking water and nutrients
  • Poor drainage creating waterlogged areas

Step 2: Scarify and Rake

Use a spring-tine rake or mechanical scarifier to remove dead thatch, moss and debris. This opens up the soil surface, allowing new seed to make contact with soil and air to circulate freely. For larger lawns, hiring a machine for a few hours is well worth it.

Pro Tip: Scarify in early spring or early autumn when the lawn is actively growing — never during drought or extreme heat.

Step 3: Core Aerate the Soil

Aeration involves removing small plugs of soil to relieve compaction and improve drainage. You can use a hollow-tine aerator (best) or a garden fork for small areas. Aim for holes every 10–15 cm. After aerating, brush sharp sand or top-dressing compound into the holes to keep them open.

Step 4: Overseed Bare Patches

Choose a grass seed blend matched to your conditions — shade-tolerant for darker spots, hard-wearing for high-traffic areas. Scratch the bare patch lightly, scatter seed at roughly double the normal rate, press it in with your foot or a roller, then water gently twice daily for 2–3 weeks.

Step 5: Feed and Water Consistently

Apply a balanced spring lawn fertiliser with a higher nitrogen content to promote green leafy growth. Water deeply 2–3 times per week rather than shallow daily watering — deep watering encourages deep root systems that are resilient to heat and dry spells.

"Most lawn failures come down to irregular watering and skipped feeding. Consistency is everything."

Maintenance Schedule

  1. Week 1–2: Scarify, aerate, top-dress
  2. Week 2–3: Overseed bare patches, first feed
  3. Week 3–6: Daily watering of new seed areas
  4. Week 6+: Regular mowing, monthly feeding

With patience and consistent care, you should see visible improvement within 4–6 weeks. For severe cases or large areas, consider booking a professional assessment — sometimes the soil itself needs remediation before grass can thrive.
